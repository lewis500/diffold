To start, recall that the derivative of a function is the slope of a line tangent to the function. Mouse over the function $y(t) = t^3 - 7t^2 + 14t - 8$ below. The slope at every point is the height of a right triangle whose width is 1 and whose hypoteneuse is tangent to the line(Verify this yourself!). Recording the derivative $y'$ at each $t$, we get a "derivative function" $y'(t)$ on the right.

What we just did was start out with a function and then find its derivatives. With a diffeq, we do the opposite. A diffeq is a statement, like $y' = ky$ or $y' = 2t - 5$, that involves the derivative of a mysterious function; and its solution is a function, like $y(t) = t^3 + 2^t$ or something, that makes the equation true.

Below, your job is to solve the diffeq $$ y' = 2t - 5.$$ To do this, you'll create a function $y(t)$ by clicking the left plot to add dots (right-click to delete). As you craft $y(t)$, its derivative function $y'(t)$ appears on the right. The goal is to make $y'(t)$ line up with $2t - 5$.
